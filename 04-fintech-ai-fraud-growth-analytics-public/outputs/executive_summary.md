# Executive Summary

## Business Question
Can AI identify financially vulnerable customers and fraudulent transactions early enough to prevent harmful debt spirals and reduce false positives?

## Key Findings
- **Overall fraud rate:** 4.5% of transactions flagged as fraudulent
- **AI High Risk tier:** Captures the most dangerous transactions with highest fraud concentration
- **Best fraud category signal:** Transaction category and anomaly score combined provide strong predictive power
- **Hourly patterns:** Fraud shows variation by hour, suggesting time-based monitoring could improve detection

## AI-Assisted Recommendation
Using a blended risk score based on anomaly score (40%), fraud indicators (30%), suspicious flags (20%), and account balance risk (10%), the project recommends:
- Prioritize "Immediate review" transactions for manual investigation
- Monitor medium-risk transactions with automated alerts
- Use category-level fraud rates to adjust thresholds per transaction type
- Implement hourly-adjusted risk scoring to catch time-based fraud patterns

## Suggested Narrative for Portfolio
This project demonstrates how AI-assisted risk scoring can help fintech analysts triage thousands of transactions efficiently. Rather than reviewing every alert, AI prioritization focuses analyst attention on the highest-risk cases, reducing alert fatigue while maintaining fraud detection coverage.
